# Framing Problems

Define why work should exist before shaping solutions.

## Use This Skill When

- The request is still a rough idea
- Problem and solution language are mixed together
- Success outcome is unclear
- Stakeholder intent needs to be captured as a frame artifact

## Inputs

- User or stakeholder request
- Relevant source material and context
- Known constraints and assumptions

## Output

Produce a frame document with these sections:

1. Source and trigger
2. Problem statement
3. Desired outcome
4. User or business impact
5. Constraints and non-goals
6. Open questions
7. Human teach-back statement (problem + desired outcome in the human's own words)

## Conversation Mode (Required)

Framing is a conversation with the human, not a one-pass extraction.

- Ask one focused question at a time
- Prefer clarifying problem and outcome before discussing solution options
- Summarize regularly and confirm understanding
- Keep unresolved ambiguity visible

Do not proceed to shaping until teach-back is complete and aligned.

## Hard Gate

Before handing off to shaping, require the human to restate both:

1. The problem being solved
2. The desired outcome

If the teach-back is missing, vague, or mismatched with the frame, continue framing conversation and revise the frame.

## Workflow

1. Extract direct statements of pain, friction, or opportunity
2. Ask clarifying framing questions one at a time until problem and outcome are concrete
3. Separate problem language from proposed solution mechanisms
4. Rewrite into one clear problem statement and one clear outcome statement
5. Capture constraints, assumptions, and unknowns explicitly
6. Request human teach-back of problem and desired outcome
7. Validate alignment between teach-back and frame before allowing shaping

## Readiness Checks

- The problem is concrete, not generic
- The desired outcome is observable
- Source context is captured and traceable
- Non-goals are visible
- Open questions are explicit
- Human teach-back of problem and desired outcome is captured and aligned

## Send Back Rules

Send framing work back when:

- The problem is actually a proposed implementation
- The outcome cannot be recognized in practice
- Key user or business context is missing
- Major constraints are implicit or contradictory
