# Paramedic Method Quick Reference

This reference distills the writing-center handout you shared into an operational checklist for sentence-level revision.

## Why This Method Works

The method improves readability by reducing syntactic clutter:

1. fewer prepositional chains
2. fewer weak "to be" constructions
3. more direct subject-verb structure
4. fewer redundant phrases

## Six-Step Checklist

Use this sequence for each sentence:

1. Mark prepositions (`of`, `in`, `about`, `for`, `into`, `through`, `with`, etc.).
2. Mark "to be" verbs (`is`, `are`, `was`, `were`, etc.).
3. Ask "Where's the action?" and convert noun-heavy phrasing into verbs.
4. Identify the real subject.
5. Remove slow introductory wording.
6. Remove redundancy.

## Worked Example A

Original:

> The author's intention of the article is to argue for the elimination of the passivity of the learner in college through using recent studies that show the value of collaborative learning in the classroom environment.

Revised:

> The author cites recent research and argues for collaboration as a way to reduce passivity and stimulate active classroom learning.

Reduction:

- original word count: 35
- revised word count: 20

## Worked Example B

Original:

> The new interpretations on canine aggressive behavior researched by Cesar Millan are actually conceived by using a method that originally was used by Skinner and the early scholars in behaviorism.

Revised:

> Cesar Millan uses behaviorism to research canine aggression.

Reduction:

- original word count: 30
- revised word count: 10
